About Me

Hi! My name is Elisabeth Dijkgraaf. I'm a Dutch girl who moved to London in September 2016. On this page you can see my personal hotspots (restaurants/pubs, markets and parks).


p.s. if you want to know more about Holland, check this website: -> Information about Holland

Drinks & Restaurants

the Blues Kitchen

The Blues Kitchen has a really nice atmosphere. Every evening they have different bands who are performing, which makes it an awesome place. For food I had a seafood platter to share and the pulled pork. Especially the seafood platter was very good. So far, I only went to the one in Shoreditch, but I already made a reservation in Brixton for next week, looking forward to that!

JAK's

JAK's is by far my favorite hotspot on a friday/saturday night. Around 8pm it's getting busy. You can have some food first (on the right side) which is fine but not great. After that you can have drinks on the left side (this is where the fun starts!). The decoration is very cool! They always play a movie on the wall in black and white (the screen is the wall which is I guess 2 by 3 meters, so big), they have a lot of tables which you can book and the music is fun for everyone, even if you don't want to: you have to dance! The only thing which is very dissapointing is the closing time: midnight.

Website JAK's

Markets

Maltby Street Market

Ok, let's be honest: I'm living in London for 6 months now and ofcourse I love for example Borough Market. But for me it was time to discover the local markets now, and one of them is defintely Maltby Street Market on a saturday. The place is very small, it's actually one street, but in this case that doesn't matter. They have a lot of different stalls with food and drinks. For the best G&T's I recommend Little Bird Gin and for food; the dumplings on the end of the street or the fries with steak on top are very good! If you're not a local yet, don't worry; a 15min walk will lead you to Borough Market!
